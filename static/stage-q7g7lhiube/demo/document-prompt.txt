You are a senior M&A partner at a top-tier firm. I'm handing you this
60-page merger agreement — Microsoft's 2016 acquisition of LinkedIn, the
actual Exhibit 2.1 filed with the SEC.

Produce a one-page executive brief for a general counsel who has 20 minutes
before a board meeting. Format: clean markdown. No fluff, no hedging, no
restating the obvious.

1. DEAL SNAPSHOT (3 lines max)
   Parties, per-share consideration, total equity value, governing law,
   outside date.

2. TOP 5 RISKS — ranked by materiality to the BUYER (Microsoft).
   For each: cite the section number, summarize in plain English, explain
   why it matters, and recommend action.

3. NON-STANDARD PROVISIONS — any term that deviates from typical market
   practice for a strategic tech M&A deal of this size. For each, flag
   "NON-STANDARD", cite the section, explain what the typical market
   standard looks like, and explain why this specific drafting differs.
   Pay particular attention to:
   - Acquisition Transaction / Competing Bid threshold
   - Termination fee amount as a percentage of equity value
   - Match rights notice period
   - Go-shop vs no-shop structure
   - Reverse termination fee (or absence thereof)
   - Specific performance remedy scope

4. TERMINATION & EXIT
   Every termination trigger, cure period, break fee, and penalty.
   Include dollar amounts.

5. OPEN QUESTIONS
   What would a diligent GC clarify with the counterparty's counsel
   before bringing this to their board?

Cite specific section numbers throughout. Assume the reader will want
to scroll directly to any clause you reference.
