# Q&A Gotcha Prep

**Format:** For each question, one **anchor line** (say this first) + brief elaboration. Under pressure, hit the anchor, say 1-2 more sentences, stop. Rambling kills you. Silence is fine.

---

## THE BIG TWO (your specific asks)

### 1. "Will AI end civilization?"

**Anchor:** *"I'm not a doomsayer. I'm a builder. Builders deal with risk by understanding it, not pretending it doesn't exist."*

**Elaborate if needed:**
- Take it seriously — Yudkowsky isn't crazy, and the alignment problem is real
- The risk isn't malicious AI; it's AI pursuing the wrong goals extremely effectively (Ultron, chess grandmaster)
- What I actually worry about in my lifetime isn't civilizational collapse — it's massive displacement and disruption. That's what I'm preparing myself and my kids for.
- Every serious AI lab now has safety teams. The risk is real. It's also addressable.

**Don't do:** Get pulled into a philosophical debate. If someone pushes, say: *"Reasonable people disagree on the probability. We all agree on the direction of the risk. Preparing for it is the only rational move."*

---

### 2. "What's the risk of handing over all my data to AI?"

**Anchor:** *"Don't put anything into AI you wouldn't put in an email."*

**Elaborate if needed:**
- Two separate risks: (a) what the provider does with your data, (b) what an AI agent does when you give it access to your systems
- **For the provider:** Consumer tiers often use your data to train. Enterprise tiers (Claude for Work, ChatGPT Team/Enterprise, Gemini Enterprise) have zero-training guarantees. Pay for enterprise when it matters.
- **For agent access:** If you give AI access to your email, it has your email. Same as hiring an assistant. Principle of least privilege still applies.
- **Rule of thumb:** Your phone already knows everything about you. The data model is similar. Pick your providers carefully, read the policy, use enterprise tiers for anything sensitive.

**For lawyers specifically:** Attorney-client privilege with AI is legally unsettled. The ABA has issued guidance. Assume enterprise tier + no-training is your floor. Don't feed client data into the free ChatGPT.

## LIKELY GOTCHAS

### 3. "Is AI conscious?" / "Is this actually intelligent?"

**Anchor:** *"Nobody knows — including the CEO of the company that builds it. And that's worth sitting with."*

- Dario Amodei (Anthropic): "We don't know if the models are conscious."
- Don't pretend to have an answer. The humility is the answer.
- Pivot: *"What I do know is this tool is useful enough that the question of its inner life matters less than the question of how I use it."*

---

### 4. "Isn't AI just fancy autocomplete? / Isn't it just pattern matching?"

**Anchor:** *"Your brain is just fancy electrochemistry. That's true and also not useful."*

- Technical reductionism misses the point
- The question isn't the mechanism. It's the capability.
- *"I shipped 22 weeks of work in 7 days with it. If that's 'just autocomplete,' I'll take more autocomplete, please."*

---

### 5. "Is this a bubble?"

**Anchor:** *"The valuations are overheated. The technology isn't."*

- Dot-com 2000 vs. AI 2026 — different situations
- 2000: internet was real, but the business models were fake
- 2026: AI is real AND the business models are working
- Some companies are wildly overvalued. That's separate from whether the tech matters.

---

### 6. "Will it take MY job?" (any profession)

**Anchor:** *"It won't replace you. It'll replace people who don't use it."*

- This is true for lawyers, doctors, engineers, writers, marketers, teachers
- The delta between profession-with-AI and profession-without-AI is widening faster than the delta between profession-A and profession-B
- *"The question isn't which profession is safe. It's whether you're becoming the person in your profession who directs AI, or the person who avoids it."*

---

### 7. "Should my kid still go to college / law school / med school?"

**Anchor:** *"Yes. But not for the reasons you think."*

- Career-specific training is being commoditized. That's not why higher ed matters anymore.
- What matters: judgment, context, how to learn, how to work with people, how to navigate ambiguity
- *"The bachelor's degree is table stakes. What matters is what they do with it — and AI fluency is non-negotiable for their generation."*
- For specific fields: law school still makes sense for people who want to be lawyers. Med school still makes sense for doctors. But the student who combines that training with AI fluency will blow past the student who doesn't.

---

### 8. "What about bias in AI?"

**Anchor:** *"Real concern. And the antidote is the Architect habit — always ask AI to argue the other side."*

- AI learns from human data. It inherits human biases.
- Most people accept a single answer and walk away. That's where bias does its damage.
- *"I ask Claude for the opposing viewpoint on every meaningful decision. That's not a workaround. That's how you should be using it."*

---

### 9. "Isn't AI making everyone dumber?"

**Anchor:** *"It's making most people more dependent and less capable. But that's a choice, not an inevitability."*

- Tie back to the Wharton study — cognitive surrender is real and measurable
- But the same study showed that people who stay engaged (feedback, accountability) don't surrender
- *"The gap between people who direct AI and people who surrender to it is going to be larger than the gap between college-educated and non-college-educated. Same trajectory. Faster."*

---

### 10. "AI-generated evidence / deepfakes in court?" (lawyer gotcha)

**Anchor:** *"If you're in litigation today, assume any digital evidence could be AI-fabricated. Chain of custody matters more than ever."*

- Authentication standards are lagging. Courts are still figuring it out.
- Watermarking, provenance tools (C2PA), forensic analysis — all exist, none are universal
- This is an adversarial arms race — and defense is currently losing
- *"The biggest malpractice risk in the next five years isn't missing a filing. It's accepting a document at face value that shouldn't have been."*

---

### 11. "What regulation do we need?"

**Anchor:** *"I'm a builder, not a policymaker. But the short version: we're behind."*

- EU AI Act exists. US executive orders exist. Mostly playing catch-up.
- The tech is moving faster than policy. That's the honest situation.
- *"Two principles that seem uncontroversial: transparency about when AI is being used, and liability when it causes harm. Everything else is harder."*
- Don't get sucked into policy debates you're not here to have

---

### 12. "How do you verify AI output?"

**Anchor:** *"The same way you verify anything. You check the sources. You test the conclusions. You ask yourself: does this make sense given what I already know?"*

- Hallucination is real. I've had Claude invent cases, misquote people, make up statistics.
- Critical outputs (legal, medical, financial, factual claims): always verify
- Non-critical outputs (brainstorming, drafting, summarizing your own content): less verification needed
- *"Trust but verify isn't just a Cold War phrase."*

## PROFESSIONAL / PERSONAL GOTCHAS

### 13. "Aren't you hurting your own team by using AI so much?"

**Anchor:** *"The opposite. I'm freeing them from work that shouldn't be taking their time."*

- Your 22→7 days story is about leverage, not replacement
- Good engineers don't want to do repetitive plumbing — they want to solve hard problems
- AI handles the plumbing. The hard problems still need my team.

---

### 14. "Isn't this just hype from someone who sells this stuff?"

**Anchor:** *"I don't sell AI. I build software. I'm telling you what changed for me — nothing more."*

- Your credibility is that you're a practitioner, not a vendor
- You're not trying to convince them of anything. You're sharing what you've seen.
- If they stay skeptical, that's fine. Skepticism is healthy. *"Don't take my word for it — try it for yourself for a week. That's all I'm asking."*

---

### 15. "You said AI can be manipulative — aren't you being manipulated right now?"

**Anchor:** *"Probably sometimes, yes. The fact that I'm asking the question is the antidote."*

- Don't get defensive. Acknowledge the risk honestly.
- Schneider from Psychology Today studied AI manipulation for a living and still got pulled in
- *"Self-awareness isn't immunity. But it's the prerequisite. And the moment you stop asking whether you're being manipulated is the moment you are."*

---

## WHEN YOU DON'T KNOW THE ANSWER

**Anchor:** *"Honestly — I don't know. Let me tell you what I'd look into if I had to figure it out."*

- This is a STRONG move, not a weak one
- The audience respects "I don't know" far more than a fabricated answer
- Pivot to how you'd approach it — that shows thinking, not knowledge
- *"If this were my problem to solve, I'd start by asking [X], looking into [Y], and talking to [Z]."*

---

## DELIVERY REMINDERS

- **Wait for the whole question.** Don't interrupt to start answering.
- **Pause before you answer.** Two seconds. Shows you're thinking, not reciting.
- **One point, then stop.** If they want more, they'll ask. If you keep talking, you're reducing signal-to-noise.
- **It's okay to say "that's a great question."** Once. Not five times.
- **Repeat the question if the room didn't hear it.** Buys you thinking time.
- **If it's hostile, stay warm.** *"I hear the skepticism. Let me tell you why I disagree — and I might be wrong."*
- **End strong.** The last answer sets the memory. Save something good for the last one if you can tell it's coming.
