# Campaign Video: Sit Down With Riya

## The Concept

Riya sits down with 5-6 staff members around the school and asks each of them one question. She doesn't pitch herself. She doesn't talk about her campaign. She just listens. The entire video is their voices. Her only job on camera is to ask and listen.

**The question:** *"What's one thing you wish students understood better?"*

**Target length:** 45-60 seconds
**Tone:** Warm, quiet, respectful, a little unexpected
**Platform:** Instagram, TikTok, school private accounts

---

## Who to Interview (pick 5-6)

The more varied the staff, the stronger the video. Try to get:

1. **A teacher** (preferably one who's known Riya a while — their presence alone says something)
2. **A coach** (athletics reaches a huge slice of the student body)
3. **A cafeteria worker** (the unsung backbone of the day)
4. **The front desk / main office staff** (first face everyone sees every morning)
5. **The janitor / maintenance staff** (often the most overlooked, often has the best answers)
6. **A counselor or nurse** *(optional — they see students at their most vulnerable, so their answers tend to be the most moving)*

**Who NOT to include:** administrators (principal, vice principal). They'll want to stay neutral in a student election, and including them creates awkwardness. Keep it to the people students actually interact with daily.

---

## The Question

**Primary question:** *"What's one thing you wish students understood better?"*

**Alternate phrasings if the primary isn't landing:**
- "If you could tell every student one thing, what would it be?"
- "What's something about your job that students don't usually see?"
- "What's one thing you notice about students that you wish more of them noticed about themselves?"

Pick the phrasing that feels most natural for each person. Don't force the same wording on everyone.

---

## Shot Setup

**Keep it simple and human.**

- Riya and the staff member sitting across from each other — kitchen table style, not interview-studio style
- Shoot in the staff member's natural environment: teacher in their classroom, coach in the gym, cafeteria worker in the cafeteria, janitor in a hallway with mop visible, front desk at the desk
- Riya barely appears in frame — maybe her back of head, or a quick cutaway. The focus is the staff member's face.
- Natural lighting when possible. Phone camera is fine. Get CLOSE on audio — a lav mic or get the phone close.

**Key shot:** Always capture at least one "listening" shot of Riya — head tilted slightly, genuine eye contact, maybe nodding. One or two of those intercut through the video do a LOT of work.

---

## Production Notes

- **Get permission first.** Stop by each person ahead of time (even same-day is fine), explain it's a quick video for a student government campaign, ask if they'd be willing. Most will say yes. Don't ambush.
- **One take is usually enough.** These are adults. They'll nail it. Don't overthink it.
- **The whole shoot should take 60-90 minutes total.** 10 minutes per person, including setup and walking between locations.
- **Do NOT prep them with the question in advance.** The magic is the pause where they think about it. That 1-2 seconds of genuine thought is what sells the video.

---

## Editing

**Structure:**

1. **Opening card (2 sec):**
   > *I asked the people who see us every day one question.*

2. **Interview cuts (35-45 sec total):** 5-6 staff members, each answering in 5-8 seconds. Cut to their face as they answer. Caption their name and role in a small lower-third: *"Ms. Rivera — 3rd Floor Teacher"* / *"Mr. Chen — Cafeteria"* / *"Coach Taylor — Soccer"* etc.

3. **One cutaway of Riya listening** — intercut somewhere in the middle. Maybe 2 seconds. Warm, attentive expression.

4. **Closing card (5-6 sec):**
   > *The best leaders don't talk the most.*
   > *They listen the hardest.*
   >
   > ★ **VOTE RIYA** ★
   > *for Student Body President*

**Do NOT add music over the interview sections.** Let their voices carry. Music can come in lightly under the closing card.

**Do NOT add captions over what they're saying** — let the audio do the work. The only text should be name lower-thirds and the opening/closing cards.

---

## Why This Works

1. **It's the opposite of every other campaign video.** Everyone else is talking about themselves. Riya's video is her shutting up and listening to the people students often walk past without seeing.
2. **It demonstrates the #1 quality voters say they want** (listening) without ever using the word.
3. **It honors the staff.** That's a good thing to do regardless of campaign outcome — and students respond to it because it's authentic.
4. **Zero risk of canned answers.** Adults have thought about this stuff for years. Their answers will be real, considered, and moving.
5. **It's production-easy.** 90-minute shoot, one-evening edit. Cheapest flagship video to make.

---

## Risks

- **An opponent could accuse Riya of "hiding behind adults."** The video's construction neutralizes this — Riya barely appears, so the accusation doesn't land. But if asked, her answer is: *"This campaign isn't about me talking. It's about me listening."*
- **A staff member might say something unusable.** That's why you shoot 5-6 and plan to use 4-5. You can always cut.
- **Administration might raise concerns about staff appearing in a campaign video.** Check first. If it's an issue, frame it as a "student project about gratitude" and release it with a soft campaign card at the end — the video still works.
