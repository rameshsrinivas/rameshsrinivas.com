# Campaign Video: Freshman-Led Interview

## The Concept

Flip the camera. A freshman (ideally one of Riya's freshman grade captains, or a freshman willing to play the role) sits across from Riya and asks her the hard questions — the ones a skeptical underclassman would actually think but probably never say out loud. Riya answers calmly, warmly, directly.

The framing does most of the work: she's humble enough to sit down and be *questioned by a freshman*, and confident enough to answer anything they throw at her.

**Target length:** 60-90 seconds
**Tone:** Honest, direct, a little vulnerable, warm at the end
**Platform:** Instagram, TikTok, school private accounts

---

## Casting

**The freshman interviewer:**
- Should be a freshman Riya genuinely knows (or who agrees to do it authentically). Not a stranger.
- Should be willing to ask tough questions without being mean. Warmth matters.
- On-camera presence should feel real, not polished. A little nervousness is GOOD — it reads as authentic.
- Bonus if they have a slightly different energy than Riya. Contrast makes the interview more watchable.

**If there's no obvious pick:** ask one of her committee freshmen. Give them ownership — they'll take it seriously.

---

## The Questions

The whole video hinges on these being *real* questions, not softballs. Here's the list — pick 4-5 total, in roughly this order:

### The Opener (pick 1)
- **"I'm a freshman. I've literally never met you. Why should I vote for you?"**
- "Why are you running again? Haven't you already been class president like three times?"
- "Why should freshmen care about this election?"

### The Skeptic (pick 1-2)
- **"What have you actually done? Like, specifically."**
- "How do I know you're not going to just make promises and disappear after you win?"
- "What's something you promised before and didn't deliver? Be honest."
- "Other candidates are saying they'll bring new energy. Why vote experience over fresh ideas?"

### The Hard One (pick 1)
- **"What's something you're not good at?"**
- "What's the biggest mistake you've made as a leader?"
- "If you lose, will you be okay with that?"

### The Closer (pick 1)
- **"Why should I trust you with my first year of high school?"** *(strongest — directly targets freshmen)*
- "What's the one thing you want every freshman to know about you?"
- "If I voted for you tomorrow, what would I actually see change?"

**Pick 4-5 total.** Don't overload the video with questions — each answer needs room to breathe.

---

## Riya's Answers — Guidelines, Not Scripts

**DO NOT script Riya's answers.** The whole video dies if she sounds rehearsed. Instead, have her think through these guidelines before the shoot:

1. **Be direct. Don't hedge.** "I won't do that" is better than "I'll try not to do that."
2. **Admit weakness when asked.** "I'm not always patient. I've had to learn to listen before reacting." Vulnerability is the single most persuasive thing a leader can offer.
3. **When asked about specific accomplishments:** name ONE thing clearly. Not a list. "Last year I pushed for [specific thing]. It happened. That's the kind of work I'll keep doing."
4. **Never bash the other candidates.** Not once. Not even gently. If asked about opponents, her answer is: *"I don't know what they'll do. I know what I've done."*
5. **When answering the closer**, speak directly to the freshman — not to the camera. Eye contact with the interviewer. The audience will feel it.

**Practice tip:** Do the shoot TWICE. First take: raw, unprepared. Second take: after she's heard herself answer once, more natural. Usually the second take has the best moments, but keep both — sometimes a line from the first take is the gold.

---

## Shot Setup

**Sit across from each other. Two-camera if possible, single-camera if not.**

- **Best case:** one camera on Riya, one camera on the freshman. Cut between them so the video flows like a real conversation.
- **Single camera:** shoot Riya's side first (all her answers), then pivot and shoot the freshman asking the same questions, cut together in post. It takes longer but looks almost as good.

**Location:** Somewhere with character but not chaos. Empty classroom after school. Library corner. Outside on a bench. NOT a podium or stage — that kills the intimacy.

**Lighting & audio:** Face both subjects toward a window for natural light. Use a lav mic or get the phone VERY close for audio. If the freshman is mumbling, the video is dead.

---

## Editing

**Structure:**

1. **Cold open (3 sec):** Freshman looks at the camera, almost smirking: *"Can I actually ask you anything?"* Riya, across from them: *"Yeah. Anything."*

2. **The interview (55-75 sec):** Cut through 4-5 questions. Each Q&A should feel like a real back-and-forth, not a monologue. Include micro-moments:
   - The freshman pausing to think of the next question
   - Riya laughing at a question
   - A beat of silence after a hard answer
   These human moments are what make it feel real.

3. **The closer:** Land on Riya's final answer. Hold on her face for 1 second after she finishes.

4. **End card (4 sec):**
   > *She answered everything.*
   >
   > ★ **VOTE RIYA** ★
   > *for Student Body President*

**Music:** Very light background music through the interview — something warm, understated. Cut it to silence in the hardest moments (vulnerability questions). Let those land raw.

---

## Why This Works

1. **It targets the freshmen directly.** The weakest voter bloc is the one that doesn't know Riya. Having a freshman on camera as the interrogator puts the audience on "their side" — they see one of their own getting real answers, and they feel like they got the answers too.

2. **The humility is the message.** Any candidate can make a video where they talk about themselves. Only a confident one can make a video where someone else drills them and they answer anything.

3. **It neutralizes the "you don't know her" objection.** The entire premise is "I don't know her — prove yourself." If Riya holds her ground, the objection is answered on camera.

4. **It's unforgettable if a vulnerability moment lands.** One honest answer to "what's your biggest mistake?" is worth more than a hundred campaign slogans.

---

## Risks

- **The freshman could bomb on camera.** Mitigation: do 2 takes, cast carefully, don't push them to be someone they're not.
- **Riya could get defensive on a hard question.** Mitigation: practice the tough questions ahead of time WITHOUT scripting specific answers — just so she's not caught off guard emotionally.
- **The video could come off as staged.** Mitigation: leave the awkward moments in. Trim, but don't polish. Real beats pretty.
- **If Riya misfires on one answer,** reshoot that one question only. Don't throw away the whole video.
