# Campaign Video: AI as Mirror

## The Concept

A student sits at their laptop and types a real question to an AI: *"Can you help me figure out who to vote for in this year's Student Body President elections?"* The AI doesn't answer directly. Instead, it asks follow-up questions — the kind that make the student actually think about what they want. Through the back-and-forth, a list of qualities emerges. At the end, the student types "I think I already know who that is :-)" and the AI responds with a knowing "I do too :-)"

Cut to the final card. **Vote Riya.**

The AI isn't picking the candidate. It's helping the student realize what they already value — and the name that fits is the one they've already been thinking about.

**Target length:** 45-60 seconds
**Tone:** Quiet, modern, a little clever, warm at the end
**Platform:** Instagram, TikTok — this one will travel because of the novelty

---

## Part 1: The AI Conversation (Screen Recording)

Screen-record a real conversation with ChatGPT or Claude. Steer it to land these beats. Below is the exact script — type the **bold** lines, and guide the AI to produce responses close to the *italic* lines.

If the AI doesn't produce the exact wording, that's fine — close enough works, and a slightly imperfect AI response actually looks MORE authentic than a perfectly scripted one.

**Alternatively:** mock the whole conversation up in Google Docs, Notion, or Canva with a chat-style layout and animate/screenshot it. More control, slightly more effort.

### The Conversation Script

**User types:**
> **Can you help me figure out who to vote for in this year's Student Body President elections?**

*AI responds (typing animation):*
> *Happy to help. Before I suggest anyone, can I ask you a few things first — so the answer actually fits what you care about?*
>
> *What matters more to you in a president: big exciting events, or the everyday stuff that shapes your day-to-day?*

**User types:**
> **Honestly? I love the big events — but I also want the everyday stuff to feel a little lighter.**

*AI responds:*
> *Got it. One more: would you rather have someone brand new to the role, or someone who's actually done the job before and knows what works?*

**User types:**
> **Someone who's done it. I want high energy, real leadership, and I want things to happen.**

*AI responds:*
> *Okay — based on what you've told me, here's what you're looking for:*
>
> — *Someone with real experience in the role*
> — *Someone who cares about the small things that shape your day*
> — *Someone who listens more than they talk*
> — *Someone who actually delivers on what they say*
> — *Someone who leads with energy and heart*

*(Each line appears with a slight delay for dramatic effect)*

**User types:**
> **I think I already know who that is :-)**

*AI responds:*
> *I do too :-)*

### How to Screen-Record This

1. Open ChatGPT or Claude in a browser — clean window, no other tabs visible
2. Type the user lines slowly, like you're thinking through each answer
3. Let the AI respond (guide it with follow-up prompts if it goes off-script)
4. Screen-record the whole thing using OBS (free), QuickTime, or Windows Game Bar (Win+G)
5. Trim the recording to just the conversation — cut any loading delays or off-script moments

**Pro tip:** If using a real AI and you can't get the responses close enough, just mock it up. A clean Google Doc with alternating "You:" and "AI:" in a monospace font, scrolled slowly on screen, looks perfectly authentic.

## Part 2: The Three Student Clips

**Riya's team shoots these on a phone and sends them to Ramesh for editing.**

The student should be:
- **NOT Riya** — her absence is the point
- Ideally a freshman or someone who credibly hasn't decided yet
- Comfortable on camera for literally 10 seconds total

### Clip 1: "The Setup" (5 sec)
- Student sitting at a desk with a laptop open
- Looking slightly thoughtful, chin in hand or staring at screen
- Natural lighting (near a window is ideal)
- **Framing:** over-the-shoulder or slightly to the side, laptop screen visible but not readable

### Clip 2: "The Realization" (3 sec)
- Close-up of the student's face
- They're reading something on the screen (pretending to read the AI's final list)
- A small smile slowly forms — not a grin, just a knowing look
- **This is the most important clip.** Shoot it 3-4 times and pick the most natural one.

### Clip 3: "The Close" (3 sec)
- Student gently closes the laptop
- Camera holds on their face for 1 second after the laptop shuts
- Optional: they exhale slightly, like they just figured something out
- **Framing:** medium shot, laptop visible closing in the lower frame, face in the upper frame

### Optional: Voiceover
- Student records on their phone (Voice Memos or similar): *"Yeah. I already know who that is."*
- Quiet, to themselves, NOT performative. Like a thought spoken out loud.
- Record it 5 times, pick the most natural read.
- **If the voiceover doesn't work:** skip it entirely and use the text-on-screen alternate ending (see below).

## Part 3: The Edit

**Ramesh edits everything together in CapCut, iMovie, or any free editor.**

### Timeline

| Timestamp | What's on screen | Audio |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| 0:00-0:05 | **Clip 1:** Student at laptop, thoughtful | Silence + ambient room tone |
| 0:05-0:10 | **Screen recording:** Student types first prompt, AI responds | Keyboard typing sounds |
| 0:10-0:13 | **Quick cutaway to Clip 2** (student reading) then back to screen | Typing sounds continue |
| 0:13-0:25 | **Screen recording:** Back-and-forth conversation continues | Typing sounds |
| 0:25-0:35 | **Screen recording:** AI's final list appears, one line at a time | Silence — let each line land |
| 0:35-0:38 | **Clip 2:** Close-up of student's face, small smile forming | Silence |
| 0:38-0:41 | **Clip 3:** Student closes laptop, hold on face | Silence, then soft music begins |
| 0:41-0:43 | Voiceover: *"Yeah. I already know who that is."* (or text on screen) | Soft music |
| 0:43-0:48 | **Final card** (sticker graphic or styled text) | Music |

### Final Card

> ★ **VOTE RIYA** ★
> *for Student Body President*
>
> *Leadership • Experience • Trust*

Use the sticker graphic file ([vote-riya-sticker-trilogy.svg](vote-riya-sticker-trilogy.svg)) or a simple blue background with white text matching the campaign colors.

### Editing Notes

- **Pacing is everything.** Let the AI's final list build slowly. Each line should appear with a 1-2 second gap. Rushing this kills the video.
- **Keep keyboard typing sounds** during the conversation sections — they're atmospheric and sell the realism.
- **No music until the laptop closes.** The silence and typing ARE the soundtrack. Music only comes in for the last 5-7 seconds.
- **The close-laptop moment is the climax.** Hold on the student's face for a full second after the laptop shuts. Don't rush to the card.

---

## Alternate Ending (If Voiceover Doesn't Work)

Skip the voiceover. After the student closes the laptop, hold on their face for 2 seconds, then cut to text on screen:

> *They already knew.*
>
> ★ **VOTE RIYA** ★
> *for Student Body President*

This often works even better than the voiceover — it's cleaner, more confident, and eliminates the risk of a cringe delivery.

---

## Why This Works

1. **It reframes AI as a thinking partner, not an authority.** The AI doesn't pick. It helps the student pick. That feels smart, not creepy.

2. **Riya never appears.** The audience fills in the name themselves — which means they *commit* to the answer more than if they were told.

3. **It's shareable because of the format.** "Have you seen the AI one?" is the kind of thing students text to each other.

4. **It lands the core message subtly:** experience + everyday stuff + listening + delivery. Riya's four pillars, delivered through someone else's realization.

5. **It pairs perfectly with Concept 1.** Concept 1 asks "what do students want?" This video asks "have you figured out what YOU want?" Together they form a question-and-answer arc across the whole campaign.

6. **It can be produced entirely without Riya.** While she focuses on the speech, the other videos, and actually campaigning, this one gets built off-band and drops when it's ready.
