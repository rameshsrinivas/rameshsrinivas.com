# Campaign "Video": Lunch Table Tour

## The Concept

This isn't really a single video — it's a week-long behavior documented through Instagram/TikTok stories. Riya eats lunch at a different table every day, meeting new students and asking each table one question: *"What's one small thing you'd change about school?"*

The *message* isn't in the production quality. The message is in the behavior itself: she actually did this. For a whole week. At tables she'd never normally sit at.

**Target length:** 15-30 second daily stories, optional 45-second compilation at the end
**Tone:** Casual, warm, almost incidental
**Platform:** Instagram stories primarily, TikTok if time allows
**Production cost:** Nearly zero

---

## The Daily Format

Each lunch, Riya:

1. **Sits down** at a table with people she doesn't normally sit with
2. **Introduces herself** and asks the table if she can join
3. **Eats lunch** with them (this part isn't filmed — it's real conversation)
4. **At the end**, pulls out her phone and records a ~15-second story:
   - Quick shot of the table (with permission — face everyone or pan away)
   - Voiceover or text overlay: *"Day [X]. Ate lunch with [vague description — 'the freshmen near the windows' / 'the senior soccer players']. Asked them what they'd change about school. They said: [the one thing]."*

---

## Table Targets (pick 4-5 for the week)

Variety is the entire point. Hit tables that Riya would *not* naturally sit at:

| Day | Table | Why |
|-----|-------|-----|
| **Day 1** | A table of freshmen Riya doesn't know | Weakest voter bloc, biggest strategic win |
| **Day 2** | A sports team table | Broad reach — athletes talk to other athletes |
| **Day 3** | A theater / arts / music table | Different social circle from athletes |
| **Day 4** | The "quiet kids" — whoever sits alone or in small groups most days | Most overlooked students, biggest impact when they feel seen |
| **Day 5** | A table of students from a different grade than Riya's own | Reinforces "president for all grades" |

**Riya should NOT sit with her own friend group for a single lunch all week.** That's the discipline of the exercise. If she sits with her friends, the story dies.

---

## How to Approach Each Table

Don't overthink it. Walk up and say: *"Hey — mind if I sit with you today? I'm trying to meet more people this week."*

**Don't** say: "I'm running for Student Body President." That makes it about the campaign. The campaign piece should be *implied* by the behavior, not stated out loud.

**If someone at the table asks** why she's doing this, honest answer: *"I'm running for Student Body President and I realized I don't know enough of you. So I figured I'd actually come meet you."*

---

## The Story Content — Daily Template

Each story post should be short, quiet, and have these elements:

1. **A visual:** table shot, food, view of the cafeteria — anything but a staged selfie
2. **A simple caption or voiceover:**
   > *"Day 3. Sat with some sophomores I'd never really met. Asked what they'd change. They said outdoor study spaces — it gets claustrophobic in the library when it's cold."*
3. **A closing tag or hashtag:** Keep it consistent across all 5 days — maybe `#lunchwithriya` or just a small sticker from the campaign color palette in the corner.

**NO vote-for-me language in the daily stories.** Save that for the election-eve post.

---

## The Compilation (Optional)

**If time allows**, cut all 5 stories together into a single 45-60 second video on the last day before the election:

### Structure:
1. **Opening card (2 sec):** *"This week, I ate lunch with five tables I'd never sat at."*
2. **Day-by-day cuts (35-45 sec):** Each day gets 6-9 seconds — the table shot, a snippet of what they said, Riya's voiceover or caption.
3. **Closing card (5 sec):**
   > *Everyone had something small they wanted changed.*
   > *Small things add up.*
   >
   > ★ **VOTE RIYA** ★
   > *for Student Body President*

**If time doesn't allow**, skip the compilation entirely. The daily stories carry the message on their own.

---

## Why This Works

1. **It's behavior, not performance.** Campaign videos get tuned out. A week of lunch stories is something students *see happening in real life*. They may spot Riya at a table across the cafeteria — "wait, is that the girl from the stories?" That's when it clicks.

2. **It directly addresses the "she's in her own clique" perception.** Five days of proof, visible to anyone who looks.

3. **Word of mouth amplifies it.** The students at each table WILL talk about it. "Riya sat with us at lunch today and asked what we'd change about school" — that's a story students tell their friends. Every conversation is a micro-campaign event.

4. **It directly ties into her speech thesis.** "Small things add up" is literally the thread of her speech. The lunch tour proves she means it.

5. **Zero production cost.** No script, no editing, no retakes. Just show up and film the aftermath.

---

## Risks

- **Someone at a table could be rude or awkward.** That's fine — don't film those moments. The story is only posted when the lunch actually went well.
- **It could read as performative.** Mitigation: Riya's demeanor is everything. If she's genuinely interested in the people, it works. If she's checking a box, people will smell it immediately. She should do this because she wants to, not because the campaign says to.
- **Stories disappear after 24 hours.** That's fine — the compilation captures the best moments for the feed permanently. And the *ephemerality* of stories makes them feel more real, not less.
- **A full week is 5 days of lunches in a 6-day campaign window.** Realistically, Riya might only hit 3-4 tables. That's still enough. Aim for 5, settle for 3.
