# Campaign Video Concept 1: "The 64K Question"

## The Setup

Riya interviews fellow students with a mix of weird, relatable, mundane questions. They have no idea what the video is actually about. The final question is the real one. Post-production stacks all the answers back-to-back for a consensus effect, then pivots to the closing card.

**Target length:** 60-90 seconds
**Platform:** Instagram stories, TikTok, school private accounts
**Tone:** Warm, funny, a little weird, earnest at the end

---

## Casting: Who to Interview

**Goal: 10-15 interviewees across every demographic.** Variety is credibility. Keep each clip short (2-5 seconds each in the final cut).

- At least 2-3 from each grade (freshmen, sophomores, juniors, seniors)
- Mix of genders, friend groups, activities
- Include: an athlete, a theater/arts kid, a quiet kid nobody would expect, someone from a different social circle than Riya's, a freshman who barely knows her
- **Avoid:** only Riya's close friends. The video dies if it looks like an echo chamber.

---

## The Interview Question Pool

### Mundane / Relatable (pick 4-6 to cycle through)

#### Food & daily life

- "If you had to eat one school lunch every day for the rest of the year, what would it be?"
- "What's your go-to Chipotle order?"
- "Coffee, tea, or energy drink — what's getting you through this week?"
- "What's the worst vending machine item you've ever bought?"
- "If you could add one snack to the cafeteria, what would it be?"

#### School life

- "What's the worst class period to have a test in?"
- "What's the most overrated hallway at school?"
- "What's the best place to nap during free period?"
- "What's the one class you'd take again just for fun?"
- "What's a rule you'd quietly delete if nobody was watching?"

#### Weird / personality

- "What's a song that's been stuck in your head this week?"
- "Pineapple on pizza — defend your position."
- "If your week was a weather forecast, what would it be?"
- "What's a movie you've secretly rewatched way too many times?"
- "If you could have dinner with anyone in the school — student or faculty — who?"
- "What's something small that made your day better this week?"

#### Hypothetical / fun

- "If you could declare a surprise holiday for one day, what would it be called?"
- "What's the most useless superpower you'd still want?"
- "If you had to give a TED talk with zero prep, what topic could you actually pull off?"
- "What's a trend you're glad died?"

**Why mix these:** The food/school questions are relatable (everyone laughs, recognizes themselves). The weird ones reveal personality and make the video watchable. The hypotheticals build warmth — they show Riya as someone who's genuinely curious about her classmates.

### More Questions (Round 2)

#### Food & daily life

- "What's the single worst thing on the cafeteria menu? Be honest."
- "Boba, iced coffee, or Starbucks Frappuccino?"
- "You have $5 for a snack. What are you buying?"
- "What's the meal you order every time even though you keep saying you'll try something new?"
- "Hot take: what's the most overrated restaurant near school?"
- "If the school vending machine could stock ONE thing it doesn't have, what would it be?"
- "Breakfast food for dinner — yes or no, and why?"

#### School life

- "Which hallway has the best energy between classes?"
- "What's the most underrated teacher move? (the kind that makes your day better)"
- "What's the one thing you'd quietly change about the dress code?"
- "First day of school vs. last day of school — which one do you actually look forward to more?"
- "What's the most ridiculous thing you've ever pulled out of your backpack?"
- "If school started one hour later, what would you do with that hour?"
- "What's a small thing the school does right that nobody talks about?"
- "What's the weirdest thing you've ever lost and then randomly found?"

#### Weird / personality

- "What's the most played song in your library right now? No judgment."
- "What TV show do you defend even though everyone else says it's bad?"
- "What's the most embarrassing thing you believed until way too late?"
- "What's the weirdest compliment you've ever gotten?"
- "If you had to describe your personality in the form of a weather event, what would it be?"
- "What's something you're weirdly good at that will never be useful?"
- "What emoji do you use the most, and what does it say about you?"
- "Describe your friend group in exactly three words."

#### Hypothetical / fun

- "If you could rename our school, what would you call it?"
- "If you had to teach a class about literally anything for one day, what would it be?"
- "If every student had to do a school-wide talent show, what's YOUR act?"
- "What's one class that should exist but doesn't?"
- "If you could bring back any trend from middle school, what would it be?"
- "You get to make one school rule that everyone has to follow for a day. What is it?"
- "If you could instantly become a world expert at one thing, what would it be?"
- "What's a movie you think should be required watching for all students?"

#### Time & memory (new category — good for warmth)

- "What's a moment from this year you'll probably still remember in ten years?"
- "What's your favorite part of the school day — and it can't be lunch or dismissal?"
- "What's the best thing a teacher has ever said to you?"
- "What's a tradition at this school that you hope never changes?"
- "Who's someone at this school — student or staff — who makes your day better just by being around?"

#### Quick-fire (these are fast, rapid-cut material)

- "Sneakers or boots?"
- "Morning person or night person?"
- "Team finals week or team project week?"
- "Outdoor lunch or indoor lunch?"
- "Group project or solo project?"
- "Assembly or free period?"
- "Pop quiz or unit test?"
- "Summer break or winter break?"

**How to use the quick-fires:** These are perfect for a fast-cut intro sequence. Stack 6-8 of them back-to-back — different students, different answers, rapid delivery. It's a montage of personalities before the video even starts. 15 seconds of pure energy that hooks viewers instantly.

---

## The Pivot Question

After the mundane round, Riya's tone shifts — becomes just a little more serious, but still warm:

> **"Okay, last one — and this one matters. What's the one quality you're looking for in a Student Body President?"**

**No list provided.** Let them answer freely. The whole point is that their answers will organically cluster into themes.

---

## Post-Production: Grouping the Answers

In editing, each student's answer to the final question gets a **caption overlay** — a single word or short phrase that distills what they said. Group them into 3-4 themes so the montage feels rhythmic:

### Theme 1: Experience & Proof

- **Experience**
- **Someone who's done it before**
- **Track record**
- **Proven**
- **Knows what they're doing**

### Theme 2: Action & Delivery

- **Gets things done**
- **Action, not talk**
- **Follows through**
- **Delivers**
- **Actually does stuff**

### Theme 3: Listening & Care

- **Listens**
- **Cares**
- **Pays attention**
- **Understands us**
- **Takes us seriously**

### Theme 4: Character & Trust

- **Honest**
- **Reliable**
- **Someone I trust**
- **Genuine**
- **Real**

**Editing trick:** Show all the Theme 1 answers in rapid succession, then Theme 2, then 3, then 4. The viewer unconsciously feels the momentum build — like a drumroll. Each theme is a verse; the final card is the chorus.

---

## The Closing Beat

After the last interview answer fades:

1. **Beat of silence** (1 second — let the words linger)
2. **Text card, white on blue (sticker colors):**
   > *"Now — who has all of these?"*
3. **Quick montage (3-4 seconds)** of Riya:
   - Talking to a student in a hallway
   - Listening in a meeting
   - Laughing with a friend
   - Maybe one "action" shot — writing notes, putting up a poster, etc.
4. **Final card:**
   > **The choice is clear.**
   > ★ **VOTE RIYA** ★
   > *for Student Body President*

Same visual language as the stickers — blue background, white text, gold stars. Ties the whole campaign together.

| # | Shot | Notes |
|---|------|-------|
| 1 | Riya walking up to first interviewee, smiling | Establishing shot, sets tone |
| 2-6 | Mundane question answers | Cycle through 4-6 different students, different questions, different locations |
| 7 | Transition beat — Riya pauses, smiles: "Okay, last one..." | Tone shift cue |
| 8-15 | Final question answers with caption overlays | Grouped by theme in editing |

| 17-20 | Riya action montage | 3-4 sec total |
| 21 | Final vote card | Hold for 2-3 sec |

## Production Notes

- **Shoot in natural locations** — hallways, cafeteria, outside, library. NOT staged in a single spot. Varied backdrops = varied energy.
- **Natural lighting when possible.** Phone camera is fine — authenticity beats polish.
- **Audio matters more than video quality.** Use a lav mic or get close. If you can't hear the answers, the video is dead.
- **Get verbal consent on camera** — quick "cool if I put this in a video?" at the start or end of each interview. Some schools require written consent for campaign content; check first.
- **Capture B-roll of Riya** during the same sessions — walking, laughing, listening. That's the closing montage material.

---

## Why This Works

1. **It's not about Riya.** For 80% of the video, it's about her classmates. That's the secret — the audience sees themselves in the video before they're asked to vote.
2. **The consensus effect is powerful.** When 10 students in a row say "experience," "gets things done," "someone who listens" — the viewer's brain fills in the answer before the closing card does.
3. **It's shareable.** The mundane questions make it funny enough to share without a political agenda. Kids will send it to each other because it's *entertaining*, and the vote ask lands as a bonus.
4. **It makes Riya look like a documentarian, not a politician.** She's the one asking questions, listening, letting others talk. That's itself a demonstration of the qualities people say they want.
